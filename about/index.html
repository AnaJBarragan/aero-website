About Us

Who we are, and what we do

Our Mission

We give real world, practical experience to uOttawa students

uOAeronautics is a University of Ottawa JMTS (John McEntyre Team Space) team that designs, builds and flies RC aircraft for the SAE Aero Designcompetition. Students choose, based on their abilities and interests, one of our two subteams — Micro or Advanced — and work across disciplines including aerodynamics, structures, propulsion, avionics and manufacturing to bring a competition aircraft from CAD model to the flight line.

Engineered to Fly

Every design decision is validated through analysis, simulation and real flight testing.

Built by Students

The team is run and staffed entirely by uOttawa students, across every subteam.

Two Competing Classes

We build a Micro Class and an Advanced Class aircraft every year for SAE Aero Design.

Leads and members across every subteam — from the Micro and Advanced aircraft leads to electronics, design and CFD.

What We Do

Design, make and fly remote controlled aircraft to compete at SAE Aero Design

Learn about aerodynamics and experiment with airfoils and avionics as you develop two competing prototypes. Optimize lift with our STOL Micro aircraft, or work on autonomous hybrid VTOL/STOL flight with our Advanced aircraft. Every part of both aircraft is our own work, from the first CAD sketch to the final flight line.

01

Design

We define mission requirements and iterate through CAD, structural analysis and aerodynamic simulation.

02

Build

Our team fabricates airframes, composite and 3D-printed parts and electronics in our JMTS workshop.

03

Test

Ground testing and instrumented flight tests validate every design decision before competition.

04

Fly

We fly at SAE Aero Design East and at local test flights throughout the year.
